If this had been an actual attack, clicking the link could have sent you to a dangerous site and potentially exposed your credentials and/or system to ransomware, malware or another cyber security threat.

When you receive an unsolicited email with embedded web links, keep the following tips in mind:

Clues to look out for
 
  • Double check the links
  • Be wary of fake URLs and login pages that look like the real deal. Sometimes they even use URL shortening services to hide their true identity. But don’t worry, you can outsmart them by hovering over each link to see its true source. In this particular email, the link is [hxxps://amazoon.site], which appears unusual as it does not match the correct spelling of Amazon. If you noticed, there is an 'o' present when it should not be. Always double-check the links before clicking on them to avoid falling prey to phishing scams.
  •  
Name of sender can trick you
Even if the email seems to originate from a trusted source, never skip the essential step of double-checking. As you go through this email, observe the name 'Amazon' – it might seem legitimate at first glance. However, upon closer inspection, the domain 'amazoon.site' raises suspicion with its intentional misspelling, echoing the same mistake found in the renowned Amazon. Stay vigilant to safeguard against such clever ploys!


General Tips
 
  • Within emails, check for the "EXTERNAL" warning
  • If the email subject line displays "EXTERNAL," the email has been received from an external address and is NOT from an internal colleague.  Apply extreme caution and verify all attachments and links BEFORE clicking.

Urgency
Be immediately suspicious if the message tries to scare you, asks for financial or tax information, or prompts you to reset a password or update an account.
 
  • Be careful of sensitive information
  • Remind yourself that legitimate organisations are highly unlikely to request sensitive data via email.

Double check the origin before responding or clicking
Verify claims and offers via a trusted website or known phone number.

Unsure and in doubt
